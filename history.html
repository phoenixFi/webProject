About Astana

The city of Astana is located in the center of Kazakhstan in the dry steppe zone. The territory of the city consists of low terraces above the floodplain. The Ishim River is the main waterway of the capital. The climate is sharply continental - cold and long winters and hot, moderately arid summers.

Key Historical Events

  1. 1830: Founded as Akmolinsk
  2. 1961: Renamed Tselinograd during Soviet Era
  3. 1997: Became the capital of Kazakhstan
  4. 2019: Renamed Nur-Sultan in honor of the first president

Some informative facts about the city of Astana

The territory of Astana exceeds 722 square kilometers, the population is over 1400000 people. The architectural appearance of Astana is made in a special Eurasian style, which reflects the cultural traditions of the East and West.

Today, Astana is a city of new opportunities and a center of attraction for active, talented and purposeful youth. The capital of Kazakhstan is the main educational and scientific platform of the country and strives to become a regional hub of knowledge, science and innovation.
